IoRT Official Guidelines

Core Members and Trainees

  • The club currently has 6 core members, with all other participants designated as trainees.
  • Trainees are required to collaborate with core members on upcoming projects. Core members will provide leadership and guidance to trainees.
  • In teams with more than one core member, they must collectively decide who will take the lead. The leadership role must be agreed upon before project initiation.
  • If a team does not have a core member, trainees must step up and take responsibility for leadership within that team.

Team Responsibilities

  • All members must collaborate closely with their team and other teams, ensuring smooth communication and project execution.
  • Every team member must contribute actively to the team’s goals and objectives.
  • Even if you are in a leadership position, ensure that every member has a voice and is able to contribute to the decision-making process.

ROS 2 Collaboration

  • Since all individual development efforts will eventually be integrated as ROS 2 nodes, collaboration is essential for achieving the club’s objectives.
  • Each team’s work should be modular, well-documented, and align with the final ROS 2-based system.
  • Ensure your code and contributions are pushed regularly to the project repository, maintaining clean version control practices.

General Guidelines

  • Respect deadlines: timely completion of assigned tasks is critical for overall project success.
  • Communicate openly: always update the team on progress or challenges faced. It is better to ask for help early rather than delay the entire team.
  • Be accountable: take ownership of your assigned roles and responsibilities.
  • Maintain professional and respectful behavior in all interactions with other team members.
  • Regular meetings should be conducted to discuss project status and future planning. Attendance and participation in these meetings are mandatory.
  • Document your work: All development efforts should be well-documented to allow smooth handovers and future work by other members.

Conclusion

As a member of Intel IoT Club, your contributions are crucial to the success of each project. We encourage all members to collaborate, share knowledge, and adhere to these guidelines to foster a productive and innovative environment.
